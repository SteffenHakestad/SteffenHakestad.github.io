FAQ

Frequently asked questions about our solutions.

Q: Traditional cyclonic separators can be prone to internal erosion issues, how does the DDS™ compare?

A: There is no comparison. There are no internal nozzles/liners which eliminates the traditional erosion potential.

Q: There is a drive towards fully autonomous / remotely operated equipment, is this a possibility?

A: Yes, the DDS™ has semi-auto and full-auto modes with options for remote operation of the equipment depending on application and location.

Q: Efficient data processing is essential to our well production team, what can you provide?

A: Real-time data and trends such as separated solids (by weight) can be sent directly from the wellsite to your production team.

Q: Separation of sand > 100 Micron is expected, what about sand < 100 Micron?

A: We guarantee 90% + separation efficiency of sand down to 50 Microns which reduces the need for additional sand filtration. Why not bench test the DDS™ against your traditional solutions.

Q: What is the main benefit of the impeller?

A: The impeller introduces highly stabilized and significant centrifugal forces to the separation process, independent to variable energy behaviour of the well flow.

Q: What is the speed of the impeller?

A: The impeller speed is variable and can be conditionally tuned to optimise separation efficiency.

Q: Is there a pressure drop in the separation chamber?

A: There is no pressure drop, nor any pressure increase.

Q: What is the solids separation capacity per hour, for one unit?

A: Normative range is 400 - 600 Kgs / 900 - 1350 lbs per hour, with capability to accommodate 800 Kgs / 1800 lbs per hour during peak solids.

Q: Is it possible to maintain the well flow when flushing collected solids?

A: Yes, the patented design of the separation and collection chambers allows for continuous well flow at all times.

Q: How long does it take to empty the collection chamber?

A: The process is fully autonomous and takes 1 – 2 minutes to complete.

Q: Can your system accept flow and solids from multiple wells?

A: Yes, and where needed two or more DDS™ units can be seamlessly coupled together to accommodate high capacity multi-well projects.
